What is the meaning of odds in probability?

The probability that the event will occur divided by the probability that the event will not occur is called odds in probability theory. However, Odds are the ratio of two fractions:

  1. the number of events divided by the number of subjects (the probability of the event) and
  2. that fraction divided by the number of non-events divided by the number of subjects (the probability of the non-event).

How to Calculate the Odds Manually with Steps?

  • Firstly, identify the number of favorable outcomes in a situation.
  • Next, identify the number of unfavorable outcomes.
  • Now, express the odds numerically (the ratio of favorable outcomes to unfavorable outcomes using a colon).
  • Finally, the result of odds for winning has occurred. If you want to calculate the odds against winning. Just flip the ratio of odds in favor of winning.

Odds Formulas

The formulas of odds are of two types. They are Odds for winning and Odds against winning. The odds formulas in probability are as follows: 

Probability of winning = chances for success / all chances

Probability of losing = chances against success / all chances

where,

all chances = chances for success + chances against success

The formula for the Odds Ratio is p / (1 - p)
