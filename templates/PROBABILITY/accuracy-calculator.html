What is Accuracy?

Accuracy is nothing but the measure of how many tests it take to achieve a correct result. And for this we use 3 different methods to calculate the Accuracy. They are:

  • Method 1: Standard Accuracy.
  • Method 2: Accuracy based on Prevalence.
  • Method 3: Percentage Error.

Accuracy Percentage Formulas

For Percentage Accuracy we have three different methods and formulas. Let us see what are they:

Method 1: Standard Accuracy

Accuracy = (TP+TN) / (TP+TN+FP+FN)

  • Where,

  • TP is the true positive
  • TN is the true negative
  • FP is the false positive
  • FN is the False Negative

Method 2: Prevalence

Accuracy = ((Sensitivity)* (Prevalence)+((Specificity) * (1-Prevalence))

  • Where,
  • Sensitivity = TP / (TP+FN) given in %
  • Specificity = TN / (FP + TN) given in %

Prevalence is the amount of population that has a disease at a specific time. This is also given in %.

Method 3: Percent Accuracy/ Percent Error

Percent Accuracy = (|(Vo - Vₐ)|/Vₐ) * 100

  • Where, Vo is the observed value.
  • Vₐ is the value accepted as truth.
  • |(Vo - Vₐ)| is the absolute non-negative.
