What is Cumulative Probability?

In probability theory, if the value of the random variables is within the range then the chance of the value is called cumulative probability. In simple words, it is a measure of the chance that two or more events. Let us see the formula notation for cumulative probability.

F(x)= P(X<=x)

Here, X is the discrete random variable.

F(x) is the cumulative probability.

And also cumulative probability is determined as F(x) = f(0) +f(1)+....+f(x)

How to Calculate Cumulative Probability?

Check out the below-solved example to improve your skills on how to find the cumulative probability manually by hand.

Example: Calculate the cumulative probability of a coin?

Solution:

In a coin we have 1 head and 1 tail.

Probability of head = 1/2 = 0.25.

Probability of tail = 1/2 = 0.25.

Cumulative probability, F(x)= P(X<=x) = f(0)+f(1).

F(2) = f(1) +f(2) = 0.25+0.25 = 1.

Therefore, the cumulative probability of a coin is 1.
