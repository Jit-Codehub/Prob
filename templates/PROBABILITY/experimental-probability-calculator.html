Formula of Experimental Probability

Experimental probability can be used to estimate the probability of an event. This experimental probability of an event can be found by comparing the no. of times that the event occurs to the total no. of trials. Another name for this experimental probability is empirical probability.

Formula used for this experimental probability is shown below:

P(E) = f/n

Where, f = No.of items that the event occurs (or) Frequency.

n = Total no. of trails.
